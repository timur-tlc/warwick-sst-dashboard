I've completed the SST vs Direct reconciliation for Warwick. Here's what we found.

Key Finding: Each System Has Blind Spots

Over a 7-day sample (19,733 sessions):

Traffic Segment SST Direct Sessions % of Traffic
Normal browsing ✓ ✓ 13,753 70%
Ad-blocker users ✓ ✗ 3,168 16%
Corporate networks ✗ ✓ 2,812 14%

SST captures what Direct misses: 3,168 sessions from ad-blocker users and China traffic. These users are invisible to Direct.

Direct captures what SST misses: 2,812 sessions from corporate networks. Their firewalls whitelist google-analytics.com but block unfamiliar domains like sst.warwick.com.au.

Evidence for Corporate User Profile

The 2,812 Direct-only sessions have a distinct B2B profile:

  • 66% Windows (vs 51% baseline) — 1,853 sessions on corporate Windows machines
  • 78% Desktop/Laptop (vs 68% baseline) — 2,187 sessions on computers, not phones
  • 60% arrive via bookmarks (vs 40% baseline) — 1,687 returning professionals
  • Only 26% Apple (vs 42% baseline) — far fewer consumer devices

This profile matches Warwick's core B2B audience: architects and designers on corporate laptops.

Recommendation

Keep Direct running alongside SST. The incremental cost is zero (GA4 is free), and it captures ~2,800 sessions/week that SST can't see. For reporting, use Direct as the primary source; use SST for ad-blocker recovery metrics.
